Problem

Engineers spent ~50% of time manually writing test artifacts in Polarion — inconsistent, slow, and impacting testing quality

Solution

GenAI platform using LLMs, vector databases, and embeddings for automated artifact generation with human-in-the-loop validation

Impact

40% effort reduction · 80% consistency improvement · 95% UAT approval · 100% adoption across India & Germany

Problem

In automotive testing, engineers manually created highly detailed test artifacts in Polarion for every product increment. The process was time-intensive and inconsistent across global teams.

  • Engineers spent ~50% of their time on documentation, cutting actual testing capacity
  • Artifact quality varied across India and Germany teams, increasing review cycles and rework
  • Manual bottlenecks slowed down testing pipelines tied to product safety
Strategy & Hypothesis
If we use Generative AI to automate test artifact creation, we can reduce manual effort by 40–50% while maintaining quality — allowing engineers to shift focus from documentation to testing.

Validated through 10 engineer interviews across India and Germany, 3 months of Polarion data analysis, and stakeholder appetite-testing before committing to MVP scope.

Key Product Decisions
GenAI generation over ISO compliance for MVP
Deferring compliance to Phase 2 let us prove value and secure funding faster without blocking launch.
Human-in-the-loop validation required before export
Given safety-critical context, engineer review before Polarion export built trust and reduced adoption risk.
Historical artifacts as embedding context
Using existing high-quality artifacts for retrieval improved output relevance without fine-tuning — faster to MVP.
Top 3 artifact types first (~70% of volume)
Focused scope maximised measurable impact while keeping initial delivery manageable.

I defined the prompting strategy — template structure, few-shot examples, and temperature calibration across 4 iteration cycles. I worked directly with engineering on the retrieval pipeline, chunking strategy for embeddings, and the human review workflow. I also established the AI output quality rubric used throughout UAT.

EdTech · HMH · 2025–Present
Custom & Premium Reporting Platform
Scaling enterprise reporting through automation — 3–4 weeks to ~1 week, $700K+ revenue protected
Workflow Automation Salesforce Jira Data Platform Enterprise B2B
+
Problem

Report delivery took 3–4 weeks due to fully manual SQL + Excel workflows, creating active client retention risk on a $700K+ revenue line

Solution

Automated Salesforce-to-Jira intake pipeline + standardised report templates, eliminating coordination overhead and manual SQL recreation

Impact

60% effort reduction · Delivery from 3–4 weeks to ~1 week · 100% AM adoption · 15% YoY demand growth

Problem

HMH's Custom & Premium Reports (CPR) delivered critical student performance data to US educational districts — but the process was entirely manual end-to-end.

  • Reports built using manual SQL queries and Excel, recreated from scratch for each request
  • Delivery took 3–4 weeks per report, causing client dissatisfaction and escalations
  • No standardised intake — requests arrived via email, calls, and ad-hoc Slack messages
  • Teams had no capacity for innovation, stuck entirely in execution mode
Discovery & Validation

Before defining the solution, I ran 15 requirement-gathering sessions with Account Managers and mapped the end-to-end workflow across 8 report types. Analysis of 3 months of ticket history revealed that 70% of requests were variations of just 4 core report types, and intake and coordination consumed 40% of total delivery time — more than actual report-building.

Strategy & Hypothesis
If we automate the intake, routing, and generation of repeatable report types, we can reduce turnaround time by 60–70% and free capacity for higher-value work — without rebuilding the entire reporting infrastructure.
